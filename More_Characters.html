More Characters

In this page, other characters that play an important role in the plot will be described, namely some of Luffy's enemies as well as some of his allies.

Allies
Name Characteristics 
Gol D. Roger
Gol D. Roger was the former Pirate King and was the owner of the legendary treasure called the One Piece. He obtained this title by conquering the Grand Line. After receiving his title, he was diagnosed with an incurable disease, which is why he let go of his crew and turned himself in to be executed. The Marines made a public execution and his last words encouraged everyone to find the One Piece and that the one who finds it will become the new Pirate King. Due to his last words, the Golden Age of Pirates began. 
Portgas D. Ace
Ace was Luffy's and Sabo's adopted brother, and biological son of Gol D. Roger. He was the second division commander of the Whitebeard Pirates and was a big target of the World Government. He ate the Mera Mera no Mi devil fruit, which granted him powers to control fire. He was eventually captured by the World Government and sentenced to death in a public execution. Luffy, however, was able to free him but as they were escaping, Ace sacrificed himself to save him. 
Sabo
Sabo is Luffy's and Ace's adopted brother and is of noble descent. He ran away from his parents and decided to set sail. His ship, however, was destroyed by a Celestial Dragon, but he was saved by Monkey D. Dragon, captain of the Revolutionary Army and biological father of Luffy. He is now the Revolutionary Army's chief of staff, and after Ace's death, he inherited Ace's fire ability by eating the Mera Mera no Mi devil fruit. 
Shanks
Shanks is the captain of the Red Hair Pirates and one of the Emperors of the Sea. He used to be part of Gol D. Roger's pirate crew, which is how he got his straw hat. Shanks inspired Luffy to become a pirate and it is because of him that Luffy now has his devil fruit powers since he accidentally left it for Luffy to eat. He lost his left arm as a result of saving Luffy from a Sea Monster. 
Trafalgar D. Water Law
Trafalgar Law is known as the Surgeon of Death and is the captain and doctor of the Heart Pirates. He has a special ability from the Ope Ope no Mi devil fruit, which allows him to create a dome in which he is capable of separating and moving anything within the dome. This skill is particularly useful for surgeries as he can remove organs without the risk of damaging the body. His pirate crew made an alliance with the Straw Hat Pirates in order to bring down Doflamingo, one of their enemies.
Antagonists
Name Characteristics 
Blackbeard (Marshall D. Teach)
Blackbeard is the admiral of the Blackbeard Pirates. He used to be part of the Whitebeard Pirates, until he murdered one of the members and stole the Yami Yami no Mi devil fruit, which allows him to control darkness. He defeated Ace and handed him over to the Marines for his execution, and now holds the title of an Emperor of the Sea. He is the only person who wields two devil fruit powers, with the second power coming from Whitebeard's corpse through unknown means. His second power is the Gura Gura no Mi, which allows him to control gravity and earthquakes. 
Doflamingo
Doflamingo was the former captain of the Donquixote Pirates and was a former Warlord of the Sea. He was the most famous leaders in the black market. He used to be the king of Dressrosa after stealing the throne from the King Riku. He has special abilities from eating the Ito Ito no Mi devil fruit, which allows him to create and manipulate thin and almost invisible strings. This allows him to control people like puppets and even move in the air. He was defeated by Luffy and Law's pirate crews, and he was sent in jail. 
Big Mom (Charlotte Linlin)
Big Mom is the captain of the Big Mom Pirates and is one of the Emperors of the Sea. She rules the Whole Cake Island, where everything is made of sweets. She is incredibly powerful, as she obtained the powers of the Soru Soru no Mi devil fruit, which allows her to extract the souls of whoever she touches and adding their lifespan to herself or anything else, including inanimate objects.
